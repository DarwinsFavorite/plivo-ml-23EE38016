The problem & the metric

At every pause a voice agent must decide: has the user finished, or just paused? We output p_eot for each pause using only audio before pause_start (causality is code-audited). The scorer sweeps (threshold × delay) and reports the mean response delay achievable at ≤5% false-cutoff rate — lower is better; the silence-only baseline is the number to beat.

Results

Out-of-fold (grouped 5-fold on turn_id, 12–15 randomized seeds) is the honest hidden-set estimate; in-sample is optimistic (scored on training data). English is a clear win (~350 ms under baseline). Hindi is a marginal win (~26 ms; ±25 ms, so it loses on some splits).

Approach

A deliberately simple, well-regularized pipeline — 100 turns/language punishes complexity:

  1. Causal feature extraction (eot_features.py) over the last ≤1.5 s before each pause.
  2. Per-language balanced logistic regression (liblinear) on standardized features — beat HistGradientBoosting on the delay metric every time, even when boosting had higher AUC.
  3. Evaluation on the real metric: out-of-fold predictions → official score.py → delay@5%. Never AUC, never a single split.

English vs Hindi — and why Hindi is hard

  • Hindi is pitch-dominated: terminal F0-fall (f0_slope_last) separates eot/hold at d=−0.54, the strongest cue anywhere — matching Hindi's verb-final (SOV) declination.
  • pitch_std flips sign: English eots move in pitch, Hindi eots go flat (hesitation holds stretch a vowel) — so it helps Hindi, hurts English.
  • energy_range_p is English-dominant; included only there.

Why Hindi resists: short pauses mean the silence timer alone already hits 850 ms; the binding constraint is EOT recall (true ends time out at the high threshold a short delay forces), not false cutoffs; and the decisive cue — the sentence-final verb — is semantic, off-limits without ASR.

Why it beats the status quo

The silence-only baseline sets p_eot=1 for every pause, so it can only wait a fixed timer (≈1600 ms). Our ranked p_eot lets the scorer pick a shorter global delay while keeping false cutoffs ≤5% — answering sooner on real ends without talking over held turns.
